Case Study: Proactive Regulatory Readiness — EU AI Act High-Risk Classification Guidance

Project Focus: AI Governance, Multi-Framework Regulatory Mapping, Proactive Compliance Program Design

1. Professional Overview

This case study documents the GRC response to the European Commission's draft guidelines on classifying AI systems as "high-risk" under the EU AI Act — released for public consultation on 2026-05-19 (closing 2026-06-23), alongside confirmation that dedicated enforcement infrastructure is being stood up.

As Senior Cloud GRC & Security Architect with an AI governance focus, my role in this scenario is to demonstrate a *proactive*, evidence-driven compliance posture: converting an 18-month regulatory runway into a structured, low-cost readiness program rather than a future fire drill.

2. Artifacts Included

Regulatory Intelligence Summary: Source analysis, timeline context, and affected-organization scoping.

Risk Assessment: Risk scoring (Score 9 — Medium) framed around compliance-debt accumulation rather than acute technical threat.

Business Impact Analysis (BIA): Financial exposure modeling against the EU AI Act's penalty structure (up to €15M / 3% global turnover) and a 90-day maximum-tolerable-delay framing.

Control Mapping Matrix: A cross-framework evidence-chain design linking NIST AI RMF (MAP) → ISO 42001 (Clauses 6.1.2/8.4) → EU AI Act (Article 6/Annex III) → GDPR (Article 35 DPIA) into a single reusable artifact.

Executive Summary: Plain-English board briefing distinguishing "nothing is on fire" from "the clock is now running."

POA&M / Remediation Plan: A 90-day, six-step proactive readiness plan including a strategic option to shape the regulation itself via public consultation feedback.

3. Key Insights

Turning Regulatory Ambiguity into Strategic Opportunity: Identified the public-consultation window not just as a compliance task but as a rare chance to influence binding interpretive guidance before it solidifies — a higher-order GRC move beyond passive monitoring.

Single Evidence Chain, Four Frameworks: Designed a combined classification/impact-assessment template that satisfies NIST AI RMF, ISO 42001, EU AI Act, and GDPR requirements simultaneously — directly demonstrating the "framework fluency reduces duplicated effort" value proposition central to senior GRC roles.

Compliance-Debt Framing: Modeled this as a slow-compounding risk (Medium, Score 9) rather than an acute threat — showing the judgment to differentiate proactive regulatory-readiness work from reactive incident response within a single risk register.
