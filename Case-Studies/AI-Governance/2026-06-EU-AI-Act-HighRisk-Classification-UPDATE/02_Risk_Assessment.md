# Risk Assessment — EU AI Act High-Risk Classification (UPDATE)

## Risk Statement
The European Parliament's finalized deadline-delay amendments for certain Annex III high-risk AI system categories change the compliance timeline organizations must plan against, introducing a secondary risk of governance program deprioritization if the delay is misread as reduced obligation.

## Likelihood: 3/5 (Moderate — governance program drift is a realistic risk if not actively managed)
- The classification criteria themselves are unchanged; only the deadline shifted.
- Organizations that deprioritize AI governance work in response to the delay may find themselves under renewed time pressure if no further extension is granted (consistent with GDPR's non-repeated transition precedent).

## Impact: 3/5 (Medium — programmatic/compliance timeline risk, not an immediate technical or operational risk)
- Impact is primarily to governance program sequencing and resource allocation, not to system availability, confidentiality, or integrity.
- Failure to maintain program momentum could result in a compressed remediation window if the revised deadline is treated as final.

## Risk Score: 9 (3 × 3) — **Medium**

## Inherent Risk: Medium
## Residual Risk: Low-Medium with active governance program management and continued board reporting cadence.

## Change Since Original Assessment
The original case assessment (see original Risk Assessment) treated the compliance deadline as fixed and imminent. This update revises the timeline risk downward in the near term while flagging governance-drift as a new secondary risk to monitor.

## Business Risk Translation
Recommend treating the deadline delay as a planning input, not a reason to pause work — organizations that maintain governance program momentum will be better positioned regardless of whether further regulatory adjustments occur.
