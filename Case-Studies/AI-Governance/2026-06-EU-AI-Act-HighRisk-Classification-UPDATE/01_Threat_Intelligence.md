## What Changed Since the Original Case
The European Parliament finalized a set of amendments delaying specific high-risk compliance deadlines for certain Annex III AI system categories. A related public consultation on implementation guidance closed on 2026-06-23. These developments modify the compliance timeline organizations must plan against, without altering the underlying high-risk classification criteria themselves.

## Why This Matters
Organizations that had been planning compliance programs against the original deadline schedule now have a revised timeline. While this provides additional preparation time for affected high-risk system categories, it does not reduce the ultimate compliance obligation, and organizations should avoid treating the delay as a reason to deprioritize governance program build-out — historically, deadline delays in EU digital regulation (e.g., GDPR's two-year transition) have not been extended a second time.

## Framework Mapping
- **NIST AI RMF:** GOVERN function — update AI governance program timeline and risk acceptance documentation to reflect revised deadlines
- **ISO/IEC 42001:2023:** Clause 6.1 (Actions to address risks and opportunities) — reassess AI management system implementation roadmap
- **EU AI Act:** Annex III (High-Risk classification criteria — unchanged); compliance deadline provisions (revised)

## Recommended Immediate Action
Update internal AI governance program timelines and board/executive reporting to reflect the revised deadline schedule; confirm whether any AI systems previously scoped as "in-flight, urgent" can be re-sequenced, while maintaining program momentum rather than pausing work.

## Risk Rating
**Medium** (unchanged classification risk, timeline relief reduces near-term compliance pressure but introduces a planning/communication risk if organizations over-relax governance timelines).
