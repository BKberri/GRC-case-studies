# Executive Summary — EU AI Act High-Risk Classification Timeline Update

## What Happened
The European Parliament finalized amendments delaying certain compliance deadlines under the EU AI Act's high-risk AI system rules, and a related public consultation closed on June 23. The classification criteria for what counts as "high-risk" AI did not change — only the timeline did.

## Why It Matters
This gives us additional time to complete our high-risk AI governance program, but it does not reduce what we ultimately need to do. Regulatory deadline delays in the EU have historically not been extended a second time, so we recommend treating this as planning relief, not a reason to slow down.

## What We're Doing
We are updating our AI governance program roadmap and board reporting materials to reflect the revised timeline, while maintaining program momentum on high-risk AI system inventory, documentation, and control implementation.

## Recommended Executive Actions
1. **Update the AI governance program roadmap** to reflect the revised deadline — Owner: AI Governance Lead — Target: this reporting cycle.
2. **Communicate the timeline change to leadership and any external stakeholders** who were tracking the original deadline — Owner: GRC/Legal — Target: next board cycle.
3. **Maintain program momentum** on high-risk AI system inventory and control implementation despite the extended timeline — Owner: AI Governance Lead — Ongoing.
