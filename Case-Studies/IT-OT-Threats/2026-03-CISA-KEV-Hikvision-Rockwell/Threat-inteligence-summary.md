Threat Intelligence Summary: KEV-2026-03

1. Contextual Analysis

CISA’s Known Exploited Vulnerabilities (KEV) catalog is the "Gold Standard" for prioritization. While the National Vulnerability Database (NVD) contains over 200,000 entries, the KEV contains only ~1,000. These two additions represent the top 0.5% of threats globally.

2. Threat Actor Profiling

Motivation: Espionage (Hikvision) and Sabotage/Extortion (Rockwell).

Observed Behavior: Automated scripts are scanning for the Hikvision /SDK/service/ path to extract user configurations.

Target Sectors: Critical Infrastructure, Government Facilities, and High-Tech Manufacturing.

3. Vulnerability Weaponization Timeline

Discovery: These vulnerabilities were originally disclosed years ago but have seen a surge in exploitation in Q1 2026.

The "Why Now?": Leak of automated exploit kits on underground forums has lowered the barrier to entry for lower-tier threat actors.

4. Intelligence-Driven Action

Based on this intel, the GRC lead has recommended moving from "Vulnerability Management" (finding bugs) to "Exposure Management" (validating if the bug is reachable from the internet).
