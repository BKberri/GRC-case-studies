# Regulatory Intelligence Summary
## Case: EU AI Act — Draft Guidelines on Classification of High-Risk AI Systems (Public Consultation)
**Date Identified:** 2026-06-08 | **Source:** European Commission, Directorate-General for Communications Networks, Content and Technology (DG CONNECT) — published 2026-05-19; reinforced by 2026-06-01 enforcement-support announcement | **Analyst:** Blaise Kingko

---

### 1. Source & Publication Date
European Commission published draft guidelines on the classification of high-risk AI systems on **2026-05-19**, opening a public consultation running through **2026-06-23**. A companion announcement on **2026-06-01** confirmed that AI Act enforcement will receive dedicated independent expert support — signaling the Commission is operationalizing enforcement infrastructure ahead of the August 2026 application milestone.

### 2. Summary of the Update
The draft guidelines are designed to help AI **providers, deployers, and market-surveillance authorities** determine whether a given AI system falls within the EU AI Act's "high-risk" category (Annex III use cases: biometrics, critical infrastructure, education, employment, essential services, law enforcement, migration, justice administration, democratic processes). This is the most concrete operational guidance issued to date on a classification question that has been a major source of compliance ambiguity for organizations building or deploying AI in or affecting the EU market.

Concurrently, revised implementation timelines confirm: high-risk obligations for systems in biometrics/critical-infrastructure/education/employment/migration/justice now apply from **2 December 2027**, while AI embedded in regulated products (robotics, industrial machinery) shifts to **2 August 2028** — both later than the original August 2026 date, giving organizations a longer — but not unlimited — runway.

### 3. Which Organizations Are Affected
Any organization that develops, deploys, imports, or distributes AI systems that are used by, or produce outputs affecting, persons in the EU — regardless of where the organization is headquartered (extraterritorial scope, mirroring GDPR). This includes internal enterprise LLM deployments, HR/recruitment AI tools, biometric access-control systems, and AI-enabled customer-service or decision-support platforms with EU-resident users.

### 4. Framework Mapping
- **NIST AI RMF:** **MAP** function (Map 1.1 — context and intended purpose established; Map 5.1 — likelihood/magnitude of impact characterized) — directly supports the classification exercise these guidelines formalize
- **ISO/IEC 42001:2023:** Clause **6.1.2** (AI risk assessment) and Clause **8.4** (AI system impact assessment) — the EU's high-risk classification criteria map almost one-to-one onto the impact-assessment obligations in these clauses
- **EU AI Act:** **Article 6** (Classification rules for high-risk AI systems) and **Annex III** (List of high-risk AI use cases) — the draft guidelines directly interpret these provisions

### 5. Risk / Compliance Implication
Organizations that have not yet performed a formal AI-system inventory and classification exercise are at risk of either (a) under-classifying a system that will later be deemed high-risk — triggering retroactive conformity-assessment, technical-documentation, and registration obligations under compressed timelines — or (b) over-classifying systems and incurring unnecessary compliance overhead. The public-consultation window (through 2026-06-23) is a time-limited opportunity to influence the final classification criteria before they become binding interpretive guidance.

### 6. Recommended Action
Conduct (or update) an internal AI-system inventory mapped against the draft Annex III classification criteria before 2026-06-23; where ambiguity exists, consider submitting formal feedback to the Commission's public consultation to shape the final guidance in the organization's favor; and align the inventory output with the existing ISO 42001 Clause 6.1.2 risk-assessment process so the same evidence serves both EU regulatory and certification purposes.
